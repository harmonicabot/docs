## What is Broad Listening?

Broad Listening turns a project into a **public page** that anyone can visit — no account required. The page shows a live **opinion landscape** synthesized from the project's conversations, and invites visitors to react to the statements it surfaced: **agree**, **disagree**, or **pass**. As more people weigh in, the map updates, so participants act on the results instead of submitting into a void.

It's the difference between a survey and a conversation the whole community can see. A survey collects answers privately; a Broad Listening page shows people where the group is converging and where it splits, and lets them place themselves on that map.

## When to use it

Reach for Broad Listening when you want to hear from a wide, open audience and let them see the emerging picture:

- **Public consultation** — gather input on a decision, policy, or direction from a community or customer base
- **Large-scale listening** — open a topic to far more people than would fit in a facilitated session
- **Ongoing sensemaking** — keep a page live so opinion can accumulate over time

For small, structured groups, a standard [session](/guides/hosts/creating-sessions) or [chain](/guides/hosts/chains) is usually the better fit. Broad Listening is for scale and openness.

## How it works

1. You run one or more sessions inside a **project** — these produce the raw material.
2. Harmonica distills the conversations into a set of short, votable **statements** and a synthesized opinion landscape.
3. You **publish** the project as a public page.
4. Visitors read the landscape and react to the statements; the page updates live as votes come in.

Because it builds on a project, everything you attach to the project — its sessions, knowledge, and context — feeds the public page. See [Managing projects](/guides/hosts/managing-projects) for the project side.

## Publish a project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the project's public settings">
    From the project page, open its public (Broad Listening) settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the page details">
    Configure how the page reads to visitors:
    - **Slug** — the public URL for the page
    - **Intro** — a short framing that explains the topic and invites participation
    - **FAQ** — optional questions and answers to set context
    - **Theme** — a category used for filtering on the hub
    - **Cover image** — an optional banner shown on the page and the hub card
  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish">
    Enable publishing. Your public page goes live at its slug immediately, and you can share the link with anyone — they don't need a Harmonica account to read it or react.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Publishing makes the page reachable by anyone with the link. It is separate from listing the page on the public hub — see [Get listed on the Broad Listening Hub](#get-listed-on-the-broad-listening-hub) below.
</Note>

## What visitors see

- **The opinion landscape** — a map of where opinion sits, showing where the group converges and where it divides, in the spirit of Polis-style tools.
- **React to statements** — for each statement, a visitor can respond **agree**, **disagree**, or **pass**. Reactions are anonymous.
- **Where you land** — after reacting, a visitor can see their own position on the map relative to everyone else, shown pseudonymously.
- **Live updates** — the landscape and counts refresh as new reactions come in, without a reload.
- **Reasoning lenses (optional)** — if you enable them, the page offers extra views that cluster participants by *how* they reason about the topic, not just how they voted.
- **A way to go deeper** — visitors can open a chat to explore the topic further, right on the page.

## Get listed on the Broad Listening Hub

The **Broad Listening Hub** is the public gallery where visitors browse published topics. Publishing your project gives you a shareable page immediately, but listing it on the hub is a separate, curated step:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit your page to the hub">
    From the same public settings, submit the page for the hub.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for review">
    A Harmonica admin reviews submissions before they appear on the public hub. This keeps the gallery high-quality.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Appear in the gallery">
    Once approved, your page is listed on the hub, where visitors can find it by theme or search. You can withdraw it later to keep the page live but off the hub.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Templates tuned for Broad Listening

Some templates are designed to elicit the kind of contested, votable statements that make a good opinion landscape:

- **Community deliberation** — for open community input on a shared question
- **Public opinion mapping** — for surfacing and mapping where a group agrees and disagrees

Pick one of these from the [Templates](/guides/hosts/templates) page when you know a project is headed for a public page.
