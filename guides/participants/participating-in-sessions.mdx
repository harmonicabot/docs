## Overview

As a participant in a Harmonica session, you'll engage in a guided conversational survey to share your thoughts, answer questions, and contribute qualitative insights. This guide will help you get the most out of your participation.

## Joining a session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receive your invitation">
    You'll receive a session link from the host. This may come as:
    - An email with a link
    - A shared link via messaging or other channels
    - A QR code you can scan
    - The link format will be: `app.harmonica.chat/chat?s={sessionId}`
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click the session link">
    Open the link in your web browser. The link will take you directly to the chat interface for the session.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sign in or create an account">
    If you don't already have a Harmonica account, you'll be prompted to create one. If you have an account, simply sign in.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill out the pre-session form">
    Before starting the chat, you'll be asked to fill out a form with:
    - Your name (optional, depending on session settings)
    - Your email (optional, depending on session settings)
    - Any custom questions the host has configured
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start your conversation">
    Once you've completed the form, you'll enter the conversation interface where you can start your guided conversation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using the conversation interface

Once you're in a session, you'll see a conversation interface where you can engage in a guided conversational survey.

### Starting a conversation

- The conversation will typically begin with a greeting and may ask initial questions
- You can start typing your responses in the input field
- Press Enter or click Send to submit your message

### What to expect

The conversation will:
- Ask questions related to the session topic
- Guide the discussion based on your responses
- Gather your thoughts and qualitative insights
- Help structure your contributions

<Tip>
  Be honest and thoughtful in your responses. The AI is designed to help synthesize insights from all participants, so your authentic input is valuable.
</Tip>

## Best practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>

## Voice input

If typing is inconvenient — on mobile, in a noisy environment, or when you just want to talk through your thoughts — you can use voice input to dictate your responses.

### How to activate the mic

Click the microphone icon in the message input bar. Your browser will ask for permission to access your microphone the first time you use it. Once granted, the mic activates and you can start speaking.

### What you will see

While you are speaking, a waveform animation shows that audio is streaming. Your words appear in the text box as a live transcript — you can see and edit the transcription in real time before sending. When you are ready to submit, click **Send** or press Enter as you normally would.

### When voice input is useful

- **Mobile** — typing on a small screen while speaking naturally can be faster
- **Accessibility** — voice input removes the need to type entirely
- **Getting unstuck** — sometimes speaking out loud is easier than starting with a blank text box

### Fall back to typing any time

Voice input is optional and supplementary. You can switch back to typing at any point — click the mic icon again to deactivate it, or simply type in the input field. The two modes work alongside each other.

<Note>
  Voice input requires a modern browser and microphone access. If the browser permission prompt is dismissed or blocked, you will need to re-enable microphone access from your browser's site settings.
</Note>

## Viewing session results

After the session, you may be able to view:
- **Summary**: An overview of the key insights from all participants
- **Session recap**: A detailed breakdown of the conversation
- **Responses**: Your own contributions and those of other participants (if visibility settings allow)

<Note>
  The visibility of results depends on the settings chosen by the host. Some results may only be visible to the host.
</Note>

### Get your conversation by email

When you finish, you can choose to receive a copy of your own conversation. Harmonica emails you your transcript — your questions and answers laid out for your records — along with a link to the session results. The results link works on any device, so you can open it on your phone even if you chatted on a laptop.

## Troubleshooting

### Can't access the session

- **Check the link**: Make sure you're using the correct invitation link
- **Sign in**: Ensure you're signed in to the correct Harmonica account
- **Contact support**: If you continue to have issues, contact the host or reach out to [hello@harmonica.chat](mailto:hello@harmonica.chat)

### Conversation not working

- **Refresh the page**: Sometimes a simple refresh resolves connection issues
- **Check your internet connection**: Ensure you have a stable internet connection
- **Try a different browser**: Some browsers may have compatibility issues

### Questions about the session

- **Ask during the conversation**: The conversation interface can help clarify questions about the session topic
- **Contact the host**: Reach out to the person who invited you if you have questions about the session goals or structure

## Privacy and data

- Your contributions are part of the session and may be visible to the host and other participants (depending on visibility settings)
- Session data is stored securely and only accessible to authorized users
- You can review Harmonica's Privacy Policy for more details
